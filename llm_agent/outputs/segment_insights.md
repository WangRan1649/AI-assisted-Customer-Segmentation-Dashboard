
# AI-generated Customer Segmentation Insights

## 1. Executive Summary

- High-value customers account for 27.7% of the user base and should be treated as the core profit engine.
- Potential customers account for 27.0%, but their low repurchase frequency indicates a major conversion opportunity.
- Churn-risk customers account for 22.0% and show historically high AOV above ¥3000, meaning they are dormant premium customers rather than low-value inactive users.
- Male customers around age 45 reach the peak in both customer volume and weighted AOV, making them the most important profit-driving segment.
- Rural high-value customers show strong preference for Books, revealing a knowledge penetration opportunity in lower-tier markets.

## 2. Segment Interpretation

### High-value Customers
Business meaning: Core profit engine and cash cow segment.  
Recommended action: Build a VIP retention program with exclusive services, premium product recommendations, and early access benefits.

### Potential Customers
Business meaning: Growth engine with large user base but low repurchase frequency.  
Recommended action: Use limited-time repurchase coupons, category bundles, and personalized recommendations to increase repeat purchases.

### Churn-risk Customers
Business meaning: Dormant premium assets with historical AOV above ¥3000.  
Recommended action: Launch a high-priority win-back campaign with electronics upgrade reminders, logistics follow-up, and after-sales recovery.

### Regular Retained Customers
Business meaning: Traffic foundation with high frequency but relatively low basket size.  
Recommended action: Use bundle promotions, cross-selling, and threshold-based discounts to increase average spending.

## 3. Priority Actions

1. Launch a churn-risk recovery campaign for users with historical AOV above ¥3000.
2. Target 45-year-old male elite customers with premium electronics campaigns and expert review content.
3. Build curated book bundles for rural high-value customers to capture the knowledge penetration opportunity.
4. Convert potential customers through 7-day repurchase incentives and category-based recommendations.
5. Allocate VIP service resources to top-tier users instead of applying broad low-efficiency mass promotions.
